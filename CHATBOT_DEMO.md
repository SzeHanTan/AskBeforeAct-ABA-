## 🎯 Use Case Demos

### Demo 1: Learning About Phishing

**User Journey:**
```
Step 1: User taps "How to identify phishing emails?"
        (Suggested question)

Step 2: AI responds with:
        • Definition
        • Warning signs (6 items)
        • Prevention tips (5 items)
        • Pro tip

Step 3: User asks follow-up:
        "Can you give me an example?"

Step 4: AI provides:
        • Real-world phishing email example
        • Breakdown of red flags
        • What to do if received

Step 5: User asks:
        "What about SMS phishing?"

Step 6: AI explains:
        • SMS-specific techniques
        • Different warning signs
        • Mobile-specific tips
```

**Conversation:**
```
👤 How can I identify phishing emails?

🤖 Great question! Here are the key signs...
   [Full response as shown above]

👤 Can you give me an example?

🤖 Sure! Here's a common phishing email example:

   "Dear Customer,
   
   Your PayPal account has been limited due to suspicious
   activity. Click here to verify your account within 24
   hours or it will be permanently closed.
   
   Verify Now: http://paypa1-verify.com/login"

   🚩 Red Flags in this example:
   1. Generic greeting ("Dear Customer")
   2. Creates urgency (24 hours)
   3. Suspicious URL (paypa1 not paypal)
   4. Threatens account closure
   5. Asks to click link

   Real PayPal would:
   ✅ Use your name
   ✅ Never threaten immediate closure
   ✅ Let you log in normally
   ✅ Send from @paypal.com

👤 What about SMS phishing?

🤖 SMS phishing (smishing) has unique characteristics:

   🚩 Warning Signs:
   1. Unknown sender number
   2. Shortened URLs (bit.ly, etc.)
   3. Urgent action required
   4. Claims to be from bank/government
   5. Asks to click link or call number

   Common SMS scams:
   • "Your parcel is waiting, click to track"
   • "Bank: Suspicious activity, verify now"
   • "You've won a prize, claim here"

   🛡️ Protection:
   • Don't click SMS links
   • Call company directly using official number
   • Delete suspicious SMS
   • Report to MCMC: 1-800-188-030
```

**Conversation:**
```
👤 Is this a scam? I got a WhatsApp message from
   someone claiming to be from Maybank saying my
   account will be locked unless I verify my details
   by clicking a link. They want my username and
   password.

🤖 🚨 YES! This is DEFINITELY a scam!

   **Scam Type:** Phishing / Banking Scam

   **Red Flags in Your Message:**
   1. ❌ Contact via WhatsApp (Maybank doesn't use this)
   2. ❌ Threat of account locking (creates panic)
   3. ❌ Asking for username and password (NEVER!)
   4. ❌ Suspicious link
   5. ❌ Unsolicited contact

   **How This Scam Works:**
   • You click the link → Fake Maybank website
   • You enter credentials → Scammers steal them
   • They access your real account
   • They transfer your money

   **What You Should Do RIGHT NOW:**
   1. ✅ DO NOT click the link
   2. ✅ DO NOT share any information
   3. ✅ Block the WhatsApp contact
   4. ✅ Call Maybank: 1-300-88-6688
   5. ✅ Report to Bank Negara: 1-300-88-5465
   6. ✅ Change your password if you clicked
   7. ✅ Monitor your account for suspicious activity

   **How to Verify Real Maybank Messages:**
   ✅ Check Maybank2u app for official notifications
   ✅ Call official hotline to confirm
   ✅ Visit branch in person
   ✅ Real Maybank NEVER asks for passwords

   Remember: Banks will NEVER ask for your password,
   PIN, or OTP via message!
   10:38

👤 I already clicked the link but didn't enter
   anything. What should I do?

🤖 Good that you didn't enter information! Here's
   what to do:

   **Immediate Actions:**
   1. ✅ Close that website immediately
   2. ✅ Clear your browser cache and cookies
   3. ✅ Run antivirus scan on your device
   4. ✅ Change your Maybank password NOW
      (use official app or website)
   5. ✅ Enable 2FA if not already enabled

   **Monitor:**
   • Check account transactions daily
   • Set up SMS alerts for all transactions
   • Watch for any suspicious activity

   **Report:**
   • Call Maybank: 1-300-88-6688
   • Tell them you clicked a phishing link
   • They can flag your account for monitoring

   **Why this matters:**
   • Clicking the link may have installed malware
   • Your device info may have been collected
   • Better safe than sorry

   You did the right thing by not entering info!
   Just take these precautions to be extra safe.
   10:39
```

## 🎬 Complete User Flow

```

   → Sees "Common Scams" and "Latest News" tabs
   → Notices "🤖 Ask AI" button

   → Sees 5 scam type cards
   → Taps "Phishing Emails" card

   → Reads warning signs and tips
   → Closes modal

3. User switches to Latest News tab
   → Sees news articles
   → Notices "AI Summary" button
   → Taps "AI Summary"

4. Chatbot opens
   → Shows welcome message
   → Displays suggested questions
   → Automatically generates news summary
   → User reads summary

5. User asks follow-up
   → Types: "Which scam is most common?"
   → AI responds with statistics
   → User continues conversation

6. User closes chatbot
   → Returns to Learn section
   → Feels more informed and protected

```

```
   → Identifies scam type
   → Lists red flags
   → Provides recommendations

   → Blocks contact
   → Reports to authorities
   → Warns friends
```
